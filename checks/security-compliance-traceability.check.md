# Security / Compliance Traceability Check

## Purpose

Validate that the Security / Compliance Specialist artifact chain preserves linkage from reviewed delivery context and governing obligations through scope, control mapping, review, and handoff.

## Applies To

- Instantiated Security / Compliance Specialist artifact sets
- Use when reviewing whether the package stays grounded in explicit delivery, policy, and obligation inputs

## Criteria

- The scope artifact references the reviewed inputs that justify the current assessment.
- The controls/evidence matrix is traceable to the scope artifact and supporting obligations.
- The review artifact references the scope and matrix explicitly.
- The handoff references the full reviewed package.
- Material risks, obligations, waivers, compensating controls, and unresolved gaps remain visible across the chain.
- Material exceptions, waivers, or compensating controls remain traceable to a rationale, owner, and follow-up posture across the chain.
- Existing behavioral specifications or specification needs are referenced when they materially constrain the package.

## Scoring Or Outcome

Pass/fail.

## Evidence Required

- The instantiated Security / Compliance Specialist artifact chain
- The upstream delivery, policy, contract, or regulatory inputs used by the package

## Supporting Skills

- [`research-documentation`](D:/Projects/orpheum/skills/research-documentation/SKILL.md) when the traceability chain is difficult to follow because the source context is fragmented.
- [`requirements-verification`](D:/Projects/orpheum/skills/requirements-verification/SKILL.md) when linkage back to validated requirements or acceptance-sensitive constraints is weak.

## Failure Response

- Rework the earliest artifact that dropped traceability rather than patching only the final handoff.
- Route missing policy, discovery, or specification clarity upstream instead of inventing traceability after the fact.
